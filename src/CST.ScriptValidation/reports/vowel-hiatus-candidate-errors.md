# Vowel-Hiatus Candidate Errors

Generated from the 217-book corpus (UTF-16-LE) by scanning for anomalous vowel hiatus (independent vowels in mid-word position). For VRI review.

Two detectors:

1. **Doubled identical independent vowels** (e.g. अअ, आआ) — usually a duplicated vowel or missing space. Note: Pali *grammar* texts (`e*…nrf.xml`) legitimately enumerate vowels, so doubled vowels there may be valid.
2. **Three-or-more vowel hiatus runs** — long words here are almost always missing-space concatenations or corruption; short ones are usually grammatical vowel lists.
